# Writing goals

Bibliogon tracks your writing the way Scrivener does — a daily habit
goal with a streak, plus word targets per chapter and per book.

## Daily goal + streak (Dashboard)

The Dashboard shows a **writing-goal widget**: today's net words
written against your **daily goal**, a progress bar, and a
consecutive-day **streak** once you start hitting the goal.

- Every time you save a chapter, Bibliogon records the day's net word
  change. The widget adds those up per day.
- The daily goal is **per device** (it lives in your browser); the
  default is 500. Change it in the small **Daily goal** input on the
  widget.
- The **streak** counts consecutive days you hit the goal. Today still
  counts as in-progress — an unfinished today won't break a streak
  that ran through yesterday.

## Per-chapter target

Give any chapter a **word target**:

- Set it on the chapter card in the [Storyboard](../books/storyboard.md)
  (the *Word target* field).
- While you write, the editor shows a **progress bar** under the word
  count, filling toward the target and turning green when you reach it.

The target is stored on the chapter (in the database), so it syncs
across devices and is included in backups — it is no longer a
per-browser setting.

## Per-book target + deadline

In the book's **metadata → Story** tab you can set a whole-book
**word target** and an optional **target deadline**. When both are
set, Bibliogon shows how many **words per day** you'd need to reach
the target by the deadline (and tells you if the deadline has already
passed).
